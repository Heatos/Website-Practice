The Main Postitions

There are 3 main postitions in Wrestling

  • Neutral
  • Top also refered to as advanage
  • Bottom also refered to as disadvanage

Neutral

Neutral refers to when both combatants are on their feet
In this position both wrestling are looking for a takedown

Top - Advantage state

Top is the position you get after getting a takedown on someone else
The goal of the person on top is, generally, to keep the other person down
And to look for a pinning combination

Bottom - Dissavantage State

Bottom is the otherside of top, it's where you have gotten takendown
The main goal in bottom is trying to better your position
Weather that be getting back to neutral or to get from laying down to on your knees

Round Setup

In wrestling there are 3 rounds
Round 1 you start in Neutral, at the collegiate level this round lasts for 3 minutes
Round 2 is 2 minutes, and one of the wrestlers can choose one of the three positions to start in or to deffer the choice to next round
Round 3 is also 2 minutes where the wrestler that didn't pick last round gets to pick the starting position
The only way to make a wrestling match shorter is to win by tech fall or by pin
And you can go into overtime if the match is tied up after round 3

Points

There are a multitue of ways to gain points in wreslting

  • Takedown
  • Standups
  • Reversals
  • Backpoints
  • Rule Breaking
Name Description Point Number
Takedown Making the oppoent go from neutral to bottom 3 points
Standups When the person on bottom goes to neutral 1 point
Reversal The person on bottom becomes the person on Top 2 points

The only other way points are given out is when one of the wrestlers break a rule by the refferies disgresstion

  • Stalling - not initating action or backing away from your opponent, this start as zero points, but after it's: 1, 1, 2, 2, Disqualification
  • Flase start/Illiegal Start Position - Going before the wissle or not starting in the correct spot
  • Locking hands - The wrestling in top position has his hands locked around the bottom wrestling without it being a pinning combination
  • Illegal Holds - the points scale up 1, 1, 2, 2, Disqualification
  • Technical Violations - Grabing headgear, improper equipment ect. Points are 1, 2, 2, Disqualification
  • Cause for Ejection - Slamming or moves that can injure an opponent

How To win

There are 4 ways to win in wrestling
By points, by pin, by tech fall, and by dissqualifaction or forfeit
To win by points is just to have more points by the end of the match, if it's tied up then you go into overtime
To Pin your opponent you have to get both their sholder blade to be touching the matt
To win by tech fall you have to be up 15 points or more, and then the match just ends
A opponent may medically forfeit or be unable to wrestle

Overtime

Overtime only occurs when the score is tied up after the 3rd round
First you ender a 1 minute round in neutral first person to score a takedown wins
if no takedowns where given then one wrestler is given choice of top or bottom for a 30 sec round, the reverse is then done
if both combatants kept their guy down or both got up then one more time breaker and one wrestler gets choice of top or bottom, if the person on bottom wins they win, otherwise the other person wins

Team Scoring

There are 3 different scores you can get your team at a duel

  • Regular Decision - 3 Team points (win by 1-7 points)
  • Major Decision - 4 Team points (win by 8-14 points)
  • Technical Fall - 5 Team points (win by 15 points)
  • Pin, forfit, and Disqualification - 6 Points
